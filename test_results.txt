### Observations

- **Raw(v1) overhead**: 12.5% larger than uncompressed float64 (class byte per value + stream header).
- **Q-6sf vs Q-9sf**: sizes differ across some datasets (20-bit vs 30-bit, both in u32 tier).
- **DriftMode does not change output size** — Reanchor, Compensate, and Quantize produce identical (or near-identical) byte counts because DriftMode controls reconstruction precision, not stream layout. However, Reanchor (346 MB/s) is ~3.8× faster than Compensate (91 MB/s) by skipping Kahan bookkeeping.
- **Lossless barely compresses smooth data** (Sensor ratio 1.0007): IdentityEpsilon=1e-9 is far tighter than smooth-data ratio variation (~1e-4), so nearly all ratios are ClassNormal with full float64 payloads.
- **Financial resists compression** (best 0.8504 at Q-3sf/Reanchor): log-normal random walks produce near-uniform ratio distributions.
- **Adaptive ε=1e-6 (u32 mid-tier, Sensor)**: ratio 0.9831. At this tolerance most ratios exceed 16-bit precision and fall into the ClassNormal32 (uint32) tier, giving ~4 bytes/ratio vs 2 for u16 or 8 for float64.
- **Adaptive ε=1e-4 vs Q-6sf (Sensor)**: adaptive ratio 0.1292 vs quantized 0.2260. Adaptive routes exact-path ratios as float64 payloads, so it is larger than pure quantized when most ratios fit within tolerance, but smaller than lossless.
- **Adaptive ε=1e-3 vs Q-3sf (Sensor)**: adaptive ratio 0.1292 vs Q-3sf 0.1292.
- **A7-3sf vs Q-3sf (Sensor)**: A7=0.1292, Q=0.1292 — both use B=10; should be equal (adaptive with Tolerance=MaxFloat64 ≡ quantized at same bits).
- **A7-4sf (B=13, Sensor)**: ratio 0.1292 — smooth data at 4 sig figs uses u8 (1B/ratio); v6 equivalent was u16 (2B/ratio at 30-bit). B=13 caps max offset at ±4096, so u16 is the worst case.
- **A7-5sf (B=16, Sensor)**: ratio 0.1292 — smooth data uses u8/u16.
- **A7-6sf vs A7-9sf (Sensor)**: 0.2260 vs 0.3742 — at 6 sig figs (B=20) moderate-step data lands in u24 (3B); at 9 sig figs (B=30) the same data spills to u32 (4B) as in v6.
